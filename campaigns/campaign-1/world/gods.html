The Pantheon of the Three Realms

Worshiped across the Mortal Realm, born of the Celestium, feared in the Underworld.

High Cosmic Gods (Core Forces)

Aurelion — God of Creation & First Light

The architect of existence who ignited the first dawn. Aurelion shaped matter, stars, souls, and divine law, bringing structure to chaos. He is worshiped by artists, rulers, inventors, and those seeking new beginnings.

Nythera — Goddess of the Void

Dwelling in the spaces between realms, Nythera embodies emptiness, entropy, and cosmic silence. Her followers believe she represents the inevitable end of all existence.

Kaelos — God of Time

Master of moments past and futures yet to unfold. Kaelos governs destiny, timelines, and fate itself. Even the gods tread carefully under his watchful gaze.

Mortyrix — God of Death

The cold and impartial ruler of the Underworld. Mortyrix oversees the natural end of all mortal life, ensuring death remains fair and inevitable.

Seraphel — Goddess of Balance

Maintains harmony between life and death, chaos and order, gods and mortals. Without her, the Three Realms would collapse into war or oblivion.

Gods of Power, War, and Conflict

Tharrek — God of War & Conquest

Patron of battlefields, conquest, and military ambition. Tharrek inspires soldiers, warlords, and empires seeking domination through strength.

Varnyx — God of Strategy & Tactics

A deity of logic, foresight, and brilliant planning. Worshiped by generals, tacticians, spies, and masterminds who value intellect over brute force.

Korgun — God of Rage & Strength

Embodiment of primal fury and overwhelming power. Berserkers, gladiators, and warriors call upon Korgun when battle demands savagery.

Bellistra — Goddess of Honor

Guardian of oaths, knightly codes, and sworn duty. She punishes oathbreakers and rewards unwavering loyalty and sacrifice.

Draxion — God of Destruction

Thrives on ruin, collapse, and devastation. Where civilizations rise, Draxion ensures they eventually fall to dust.

Gods of Nature & Life

Sylphaelis — Goddess of Forests

Protector of ancient groves, wildlife, and sacred trees. Revered by elves, druids, and those sworn to defend the wild.

Thorne — God of Beasts & Survival

Patron of hunters, predators, and the brutal laws of nature. Represents instinct, endurance, and the strength to survive.

Myrienna — Goddess of Growth & Renewal

Bringer of fertility, rebirth, and flourishing life. Farmers and healers pray for her blessings of abundance and recovery.

Oakenmar — God of Stone & Mountains

An ancient earth guardian embodying endurance, patience, and strength. His presence is felt in mountains, caverns, and stone halls.

Vaelora — Goddess of Rivers & Rain

Controls storms, tides, and rainfall. She governs both gentle life-giving waters and destructive floods.

Gods of Knowledge, Magic, and Secrets

Arcaneth — God of Magic

The source of arcane energy and forbidden spellcraft. Wizards and scholars study his power with reverence and fear.

Lumeris — Goddess of Knowledge

Preserver of history, memory, and learning. Libraries, universities, and archives serve as her temples.

Nyssara — Goddess of Secrets

Knows truths hidden even from the gods. Worshiped by spies, conspirators, and those who trade in whispers.

Quillan — God of Writing & Records

Ensures knowledge endures through scrolls, books, and recorded truth. Patron of scribes, chroniclers, and historians.

Tovrix — God of Lies & Trickery

A cunning deceiver who bends truth and reshapes perception. Rogues, illusionists, and pranksters pray in secret.

Gods of Emotion, Mortals, and Dreams

Elyndra — Goddess of Love

Blesses romance, devotion, passion, and heartbreak. Couples pray to her for harmony and union.

Fenric — God of Freedom

Patron of rebels, wanderers, escaped slaves, and free spirits who refuse oppression.

Halcyra — Goddess of Hope

A beacon in times of despair. Her followers bring comfort, courage, and light in dark times.

Morvane — God of Despair

Feeds on suffering, grief, and broken souls. Often feared as a corrupting and manipulative force.

Pheron — God of Dreams

Walks through sleeping minds, shaping visions, prophecies, nightmares, and subconscious worlds.

Gods of Death & the Underworld

Velkaris — Judge of Souls

Determines the fate of spirits after death, weighing mortal virtue and sin.

Noxara — Goddess of Ghosts

Rules lost souls, specters, and spirits who cannot move on.

Threnos — God of Mourning

Embodies grief, remembrance, and the emotional burden of loss.

Calyrix — God of Rebirth

Returns souls to new life through reincarnation, renewal, or divine resurrection.

Umbros — God of Darkness

Dwells in shadow between worlds, feared as a being of night, mystery, and hidden power.

Gods of Movement, Craft, and Civilization

Aerthys — Goddess of Wind & Speed

Patron of travelers, messengers, freedom, and swift movement across land and sky.

Ferrum — God of Smithing & Craft

Creator of legendary weapons, armor, and masterwork creations. Blacksmiths honor him at their forges.

Marros — God of Trade & Wealth

Oversees commerce, fortune, and prosperity. Merchants pray for profit and luck.

Kaedrin — God of Roads & Travel

Guides explorers, caravans, and pilgrims on long and dangerous journeys.

Oruneth — God of Fate & Destiny

Weaves mortal paths and divine prophecy, guiding destinies beyond mortal understanding.
